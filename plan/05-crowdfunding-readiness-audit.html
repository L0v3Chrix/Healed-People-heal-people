Readiness is a kindness. Launching unready wastes the gifts people give.

This document serves future supporters — it refuses to ask for anyone's money before the foundations deserve it.

Where the campaign stands today

Readiness audit, not launch plan

This is an evidence and asset readiness audit, not the final crowdfunding plan.

Still interview decisions

Platform, goal, funding model, rewards, dates, financial claims, partner claims, and fulfillment commitments remain interview decisions.

The story the assets already tell

Five honest beats, no invented claims

The reviewed assets support a clear campaign narrative without inventing claims.

  1. The problem with the usual frame

    People experiencing homelessness are often positioned only as recipients.

  2. The Worth Keeping inversion

    The distributor gives a collectible sticker first.

  3. The three-way exchange

    The recipient receives art and a mystery local reward; the business receives a visit and measurable participation; the distributor participates as a contributor.

  4. The accountable trigger

    Verified redemption—not scanning alone—is the event intended to create sponsor-funded accounting.

  5. The long view

    A physical exchange can make community participation visible without pity-driven imagery or language.

Hope, framed honestly

This narrative is suitable for campaign development only if the final page keeps future outcomes framed as intended or proposed until pilot evidence exists.

About the JPEG derivatives

The optimized JPEG files in the static explainer are derivatives of the PNG set, not additional creative concepts. They should not inflate the campaign asset count.

Generated-visual release rule

Before any generated visual is published, a human owner must verify visible words, numbers, QR codes, logos, apparent people, locations, rights, and implied operations. Decorative QR codes must be removed or replaced with controlled test/production codes; exact-looking metrics must be removed or explicitly labeled illustrative; generated portraits must not be represented as real participants.

Numbers and claims we will not present as fact

Existing material that stays illustrative until proven

None of the following may appear in public campaign material as established fact.

  • $2 pledge amount
  • $1.60 / $0.40 split
  • 500-sticker drop
  • 50-sticker packs
  • 70 / 20 / 8 / 2 rarity distribution
  • 20% overhead, seven-day review, and 90-day retention
  • 5–10 distributors, 1–3 businesses/sponsors, or 500–1,000 stickers
  • “Instant” participant disbursement
  • Persona names or stories presented as real participants
  • Partner-like names, logos, quotes, or affiliations
  • Redemption, business, sponsor, distributor, or community metrics
  • “Micro-grant,” “payout,” or tax-deductibility language before authorized determination
  • A specific Austin launch, date, neighborhood, or organization before commitment
  • A production or delivery date without a costed printer and fulfillment schedule
  • Any QR code shown as functional before code generation, collision, print, and destination testing

Trust is the only currency this campaign can't refund.

Each cluster below waits on interview decisions before its assets can exist honestly.

No reward ships until it clears this gate

Eight conditions, all required

No reward should appear on the campaign page until all of these are true.

  • It is consistent with the mission and approved sticker-ownership rule.
  • It does not divert inventory or funds needed for the beta’s participant commitments.
  • Its unit economics include packaging, fees, postage, taxes, loss, replacement, and support.
  • A vendor has confirmed quantity, quality, timing, and reprint terms.
  • The promised delivery date includes proofing, manufacturing, inbound freight, quality control, packing, and contingency.
  • The reward can be fulfilled without exposing participant identity or sensitive data.
  • Any scarcity, rarity, artist, partner, environmental, or impact claim is documented.
  • The operating entity and campaign platform permit it.

Choose the honest rung on the proof ladder

Five levels of evidence, one truthful claim

The interview should select the honest proof level the campaign can reach before launch.

  1. Concept proof

    Narrative prototype and illustrative sticker art only

  2. Physical proof

    Manufactured samples with tested scans

  3. Workflow proof

    Controlled end-to-end demonstration with a test business/reward

  4. Partner proof

    Written operating commitment from a qualified community partner and business

  5. Pilot proof

    Real, consented, reconciled pilot activity with evidence-backed outcomes

Say exactly where we stand

The campaign page must state the selected level accurately. It should never visually imply a higher proof level than the evidence supports.

The final gate before the campaign goes public

Ten checks, all open until approved

The campaign is not ready to publish until every box below is checked.

  • Operating entity and authorized campaign owner are confirmed
  • Campaign purpose and proof level are approved
  • Goal is built from sourced costs rather than a desired headline number
  • Funds custody, distributor-support classification, accounting, and disclosures are approved
  • Partner, participant, business, quote, image, and logo permissions are documented
  • Rewards and fulfillment have cost, vendor, schedule, privacy, and support owners
  • Every public claim has evidence and an approver
  • Page, checkout, video, captions, transcript, mobile layout, links, and emails are tested
  • Refund, delay, shortfall, overfunding, and scope-change responses are approved
  • Launch-day roles, access, monitoring, support, and update cadence are assigned

Why we hold the line: a campaign built on verified evidence honors every person it claims to serve. When these boxes are checked, we launch — and not a day sooner.
