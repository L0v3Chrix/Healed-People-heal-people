The idea, in one honest readout

Executive readout — what StickerPass is and where it truly stands

StickerPass is a dignity-first civic commerce system — and the giving starts with the person you'd least expect.

A person experiencing homelessness distributes a collectible, platform-owned sticker and becomes the first giver. A recipient scans it, unlocks a mystery local reward, and redeems that reward at a participating business. The verified redemption is the only event that may trigger sponsor-funded accounting and support for the distributor.

🤝The distributor

Participates as a contributor rather than a passive object of charity.

🎁The recipient

Receives a collectible and a local experience.

🏪The business

Receives foot traffic and measurable civic participation.

The supplied work already contains a strong narrative system, a cohesive visual identity, a public story prototype, a buildable marketing prototype, two static planning/pitch artifacts, and a substantial local database design. It does not yet establish the legal, financial, operational, safety, production, pilot, or crowdfunding decisions required for a production beta.

The one truth to hold onto

Static schema and prototype depth should not be confused with a deployed transactional product.

Seven principles the concept has already confirmed

Confirmed concept principles — the non-negotiables of the design

Every downstream decision in this package is checked against these seven confirmed principles.

  • Dignity inversion is the core mechanism. The person distributing the sticker gives first.
  • StickerPass is not primarily a coupon app or a conventional fundraiser. The collectible object and exchange create the relationship.
  • All three participants should receive clear value. Distributor, recipient, and participating business must each benefit.
  • Verified redemption is the money-moving event. A scan or claim alone does not authorize a financial outcome.
  • The public story must avoid pity, savior language, exploitation, and crisis imagery. It should be plainspoken, eye-level, and human.
  • The sticker is treated as a collectible pass. Rarity, tactile print qualities, peeling, QR scanning, Austin character, and pass-line motifs are established creative directions.
  • Early financial operations may be human-executed. The existing roadmap explicitly avoids week-one payment integration and instead computes accountable amounts for people to execute.

People don't care how much you know till they know how much you care.

Seven tensions that need a decision

Product and operating tensions — where the concepts honestly disagree

The supplied materials pull in different directions on seven questions; naming each tension now prevents an accidental commitment later.

🏷️1 · Sticker ownership vs. sponsor branding

One concept positions the sticker as a platform-owned collectible without a business logo. Another presents a business-facing Studio that may imply customized or business-branded creation. The beta needs one explicit rule for ownership, visible branding, sponsor attribution, and creative control.

⚖️2 · "Micro-grant" vs. "payout"

The materials use both terms. They may imply materially different legal, tax, accounting, eligibility, and participant-expectation consequences. Final product language and database states cannot be approved until qualified guidance and the operating entity choose the correct model.

🔄3 · Scan, claim, unlock, and redemption

The story shows a scan unlocking a reward, while the financial model makes verified redemption the trigger. The beta needs an exact state machine: what is public, what creates a claim, whether a reward can expire, who verifies redemption, how duplicate or failed events resolve, and when money is recorded.

✍️4 · Manual execution vs. product promises

Manual execution is viable for a controlled pilot, but the product must disclose processing expectations and provide reconciliation, exception handling, audit evidence, and human authority. A computed ledger is not the same as delivered funds.

📖5 · Storytelling vs. product UX

The existing pages explain the idea well, but they do not establish the authenticated workflows required by distributors, partner organizations, businesses, sponsors, operators, or auditors.

🗄️6 · Roadmap claims vs. database enforcement

The roadmap describes automatic two-row ledger creation after a verified redemption. The migration contains mutation guards but no redemption-to-ledger trigger. Atomic offer-cap consumption, per-redemption split uniqueness, idempotency, distributor authentication, payout-to-ledger referential integrity, audit logging, retention, and daily ceilings are also not database-enforced in the recovered schema.

🖼️7 · Public graphics vs. operating truth

One generated graphic promises an instant distributor micro-grant while the roadmap and explainer require manual execution after human review. Four separately named community/hero assets are also byte-identical. Campaign production must replace or intentionally relabel those assets rather than present them as distinct evidence.

What the interview must decide

Interview decision agenda — seven clusters of open questions

Every open decision from discovery was routed into one of seven interview clusters, so nothing important could slip through as an assumption.

🎯Mission and beta outcome

  • The single most important real-world proof the beta must produce
  • Pilot geography, cohort size, dates, duration, and success thresholds
  • Whether the beta includes real money, real rewards, physical stickers, or controlled simulations

🏛️Governance and authority

  • Operating entity: independent nonprofit, fiscal sponsor, benefit company, or another approved arrangement
  • Named decision owner for product, money, safety, legal, partner approval, and public claims
  • Authorized partner organizations and their operating responsibilities

🧍Participant model

  • Eligibility, onboarding, consent, identity requirements, training, safety, support, suspension, and exit for distributors
  • Business and sponsor qualification, agreements, reward rules, service levels, and offboarding
  • Recipient age rules, accessibility, privacy, and customer support

💵Financial model

  • Correct classification and public name for distributor support
  • Funding source, amount formula, overhead, caps, timing, payout rail, exceptions, refunds, chargebacks, abandoned balances, tax treatment, and reporting
  • Who holds funds and who is legally permitted to authorize and execute transfers

📦Physical product and fulfillment

  • Sticker construction, dimensions, QR durability, serialization, batch size, rarity, packaging, printer, quality control, storage, shipping, replacement, and damaged/unused inventory policy

📣Crowdfunding

  • Campaign purpose, audience, platform, funding model, goal, minimum viable raise, use of funds, launch timing, proof standard, reward philosophy, production promises, fulfillment owner, budget, and risk disclosures

🧰Team and delivery constraints

  • Available budget, people, skills, existing accounts, required dates, preferred vendors, data residency, support hours, accessibility target, and approval cadence

Why this is ready for structured definition

Discovery conclusion — coherent enough to proceed, honest about what's next

The concept is sufficiently coherent to proceed to structured definition — the creative thesis and narrative are stronger than the operating specification.

The interview should therefore focus first on the beta proof, governance, participant safeguards, money flow, and fulfillment. Those answers will determine which existing technical work is retained and prevent the final roadmap from turning illustrative concepts into accidental commitments.
