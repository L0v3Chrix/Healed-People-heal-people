Pick a funding family before picking perks

Four fundamentally different ways to fund this

Every one of the 35 options belongs to one of four families — and the family choice comes first.

🎁1. Reward/preorder crowdfunding

Supporters receive recognition, access, or goods, but no ownership or financial return.

🤝2. Mission and business sponsorship

Supporters or businesses fund defined pilot activities and receive approved recognition, participation, or advertising value; this is not automatically a charitable donation or tax-deductible.

📈3. Regulated investment crowdfunding

Investors purchase a security through a compliant offering and may receive a financial return only according to the instrument and business performance. They can lose their entire investment.

🔁4. Sequenced hybrid

Build interest and physical proof first, then run one regulated investment offering with a small set of mission-aligned investor perks. A reward purchase and a security must remain clearly distinguishable.

Three coherent campaign architectures

How the 35 options combine into one honest campaign

Rather than picking perks piecemeal, choose one of three whole-campaign shapes — each with its own strengths and obligations.

  1. Architecture 1 — Rewards-first proof campaign

    Use approximately five to eight options from 1–28. This is simpler to explain but provides no financial return and creates physical-fulfillment obligations. It is strongest if the immediate objective is funding a demonstrable product and physical proof.

  2. Architecture 2 — Wefunder Community Round

    Use one approved security from 29–34, optionally combined with a restrained perk ladder based on option 35. This matches the for-profit direction and the desire to let the community participate in potential enterprise value, but it requires corporate, accounting, securities, disclosure, investor-relations, and ongoing-reporting readiness.

  3. Architecture 3 — Sequenced hybrid (current recommendation for investigation)

    First complete entity/financial readiness and build a warm audience around the prototype and physical samples without selling securities prematurely. Then launch a Wefunder Community Round using one clearly explained security and five or fewer costed investor perks. Retain options 24–28 as separate business sales/sponsorship packages rather than confusing them with investor terms. This preserves the community-ownership idea while keeping the campaign understandable.

Current official constraints to carry into the decision

The regulatory facts as they stand today

These constraints come from official sources and must travel with any funding-family decision.

  • Under Regulation Crowdfunding, an eligible company may currently raise up to $5 million across Regulation Crowdfunding offerings in a rolling 12-month period, and the transaction must be conducted through one registered intermediary. The issuer must make required disclosures, including offering terms, use of proceeds, business and ownership information, risks, and financial statements; ongoing annual reporting can apply.
  • Wefunder currently describes its public Community Round as open to accredited and non-accredited investors, publicly promotable, and capable of supporting multiple investment-contract types. Its published Community Round price is 7.9% of successfully raised funds plus an annual administrative fee equal to the lesser of $1,000 or 0.5% of the amount raised; LLC structure and tax treatment can create additional costs.
  • Wefunder currently lists C corporations, LLCs, and U.S. public benefit corporations as supported structures. It publishes SAFE, convertible-note, equity, revenue-share, simple-loan, and custom-contract pathways, subject to the applicable offering and platform review.

The promise we will never make

No StickerPass investor should be promised that money will be returned or that a profit will occur. Startup securities are risky and often illiquid.

The decision rule

Family first, then a short honest ladder

One rule keeps this whole menu from becoming a mess of promises.

Do not choose individual perks until the funding family is selected. After that choice, shortlist a maximum of eight outward-facing offers for cost and feasibility analysis, and use only one security instrument in an investment offering unless counsel and the platform approve a more complex structure.
