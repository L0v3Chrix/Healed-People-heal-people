A promise is only kind if it names the gate it must pass through.

This document serves everyone waiting on a deliverable — it shows exactly which gate each promise sits behind, so nobody is left guessing.

Why this map exists

Guardrails for every promise we put on paper

This map prevented unsupported assumptions from entering the final production roadmap.

The interview is closed. Unresolved regulated or vendor-dependent matters now appear as explicit release gates in documents 12 through 21 — nothing quietly slipped through as a "requirement" without earning it.

Care shows up as honesty

Every deliverable below is split into two honest columns: what the evidence already supports, and what still had to be settled by real people making real decisions. That's how we keep the plan worthy of the community it serves.

How the final package will be assembled

Eleven copy-ready sections, in order

After interview completion and full asset hydration/review, the final package will use these separate copy-ready sections.

  1. Pre-Design Review (PDR)
  2. Project Scope
  3. Technical Requirements Specification
  4. Recommended Tech Stack
  5. Required Assets
  6. Platforms
  7. Subscriptions and Services
  8. Crowdfunding Plan, Assets, and Recommendations
  9. Required Tools and Installation/Setup
  10. Required Skills and Training/Resources
  11. Traceability and Consistency Matrix

Every requirement must earn its place

The traceability rule

Every final requirement or recommendation must cite one of three legitimate sources — no exceptions.

  • A reviewed supplied asset
  • A confirmed interview answer
  • A professional determination explicitly commissioned and approved during the interview
Anything else is excluded

Anything else must be excluded or clearly presented as an option requiring a decision — never as a project requirement.

The point of the map: nothing reaches the community as a commitment until a person with the authority to make it — and the evidence to back it — has said yes. Care means never letting a guess wear the costume of a fact.
