Every asset gets an owner before it gets an audience

The asset rule

Nothing goes public, or into operations, until someone is accountable for it.

The rule, in full

Every public or operational asset needs an owner, source file, rights status, approver, version, accessibility variant, and claims check. "Existing" means usable as input, not automatically publishable.

Existing is a starting line, not a finish line.

Design every screen someone might meet — even the hard ones

Product UX and content

Every person who touches Worth Keeping — recipient, distributor, partner, business, operator — deserves a screen that was designed for their moment.

📱Recipient scan & claim

Public scan, invalid/void/already-claimed/rate-limited, reveal, claim, expiry, redemption, correction, and help screens.

🤝Optional preference profile

Purpose-specific consent, skip, edit, withdraw, export, and delete paths.

🎒Distributor experience

Onboarding, training, consent, inventory, activity/value statement, support, complaint, appeal, and exit screens plus printable equivalents.

🏛️Partner tools

Dashboard, participant/pack workflows, restricted incident/support case views, and operating reports.

🏪Business workflows

Onboarding, approved offer templates, custom review, location/staff access, redemption, correction/dispute, funding/status, and reports.

🎛️Operations dashboards

Operator, finance, support, claims, fraud, inventory, audit, reconciliation, incident, and release dashboards.

  • Empty, loading, offline, error, timeout, retry, unauthorized, suspended, and maintenance states — designed, not defaulted.
  • A plain-language glossary replacing "micro-grant," "payout," "pledge," or "overhead" after professional determination.

Write down how the program actually governs itself

Operational and governance assets

Trust at this scale is paperwork done with heart — agreements, policies, and procedures that protect every party before anything goes wrong.

🏢Entity and authority

Entity/cap-table/authority record and responsibility matrix.

✍️Agreements

Community-partner agreement, business/sponsor agreement, data-processing terms, artist/vendor agreement, and print purchase order.

🫶Participant policies

Eligibility, consent, privacy, training, safety, incident, media, complaint, suspension, appeal, and exit policies.

🏬Business policies

Qualification, allowed/prohibited offer, age/restriction, verification, dispute, refund, expiry, and offboarding policies.

💵Financial SOPs

Financial formula, custody, chart of accounts, approval, execution, reconciliation, correction, refund, chargeback, failure, tax-document, and retention SOPs.

🔐Data and security

Role/access matrix, data inventory/map, retention schedule, privacy notice, terms, cookie/analytics statement, security plan, incident response, backup/restore, vendor register, and access review.

☎️Support readiness

Support scripts, escalation map, service windows, emergency language, and printable contact card.

📢Public claims control

Claims register and public-report approval workflow.

Learn honestly from the beta before telling its story

Beta research and reporting assets

The pilot only teaches us something if we plan how to listen — and only earns trust if we report what we actually saw.

  • Research plan for distributors, recipients, partner staff, businesses, sponsors, and operators.
  • Voluntary research consent separate from program participation.
  • Observation/interview guides and compensation decision.
  • Metrics dictionary with numerator, denominator, source, owner, exclusion, and privacy threshold.
  • Daily operations dashboard, weekly reconciliation, safety/incident review, pilot close report, and continue/revise/pause/stop memo.
  • Public impact report template using aggregate evidence only.
Consent is never the price of help

Research consent stays voluntary and separate from program participation. Nobody trades their story for their sticker.

Build the campaign on proof, not polish

Crowdfunding assets

Everything an investor sees must be honest about how far along we actually are — no borrowed credibility, no implied outcomes.

Page and investment package

  • Final campaign name, one-line promise, short description, and honest proof-level statement.
  • Wefunder company profile, investment terms, Form C inputs, capitalization/ownership, financial statements, risks, use of proceeds, minimum/maximum, deadline, oversubscription logic, and investor FAQ.
  • Plain-language explanation of the selected security and explicit total-loss/illiquidity risk.
  • Founder/team bios, portraits, roles, relevant experience, conflicts, and approved contact details.
  • Product architecture diagram, three-party value diagram, physical/digital lifecycle, accountability flow, roadmap, budget chart, and risk/mitigation graphic.
  • Investor-perk table with cost, quantity, cumulative logic, delivery, geography, tax/shipping, and limitations.

Media

  • Founder campaign film; 60–90 second primary cut plus short social cuts.
  • Product demo showing controlled sample scan, reveal, staff verification, and accountability workflow.
  • Real physical sticker photography and scan footage.
  • Austin establishing visuals that do not imply unapproved organizations or participant outcomes.
  • Participant/community footage only with voluntary, revocable, non-coercive media consent; create a complete campaign version that needs no vulnerable-person footage.
  • Captions, transcript, alt text, audio description where necessary, thumbnail, poster frame, and mobile-safe crops.
Dignity rule for the camera

Participant and community footage requires voluntary, revocable, non-coercive media consent — and the campaign must have a complete version that needs no vulnerable-person footage at all.

Outreach and operations

  • Investor Warmup page, consented email capture, welcome/education/nurture/launch sequences.
  • Press release, one-page fact sheet, press FAQ, approved images, founder bio, media contact, correction protocol.
  • Creator/community/business outreach scripts and tracker.
  • Launch-day runbook, response library, moderation rules, investor update template, delay/scope-change/refund communications, and post-close onboarding.

Ship the Infinity Page — the whole plan on one screen

Infinity Page deliverable

One standalone, responsive HTML infographic that lets anyone see the entire plan — no login, no dependencies, no spin.

Create one standalone responsive HTML infographic at deliverables/stickerpass-infinity-page/index.html with supporting local CSS/JS/assets. It must visually summarize:

  1. The concept

    The concept and three-party exchange.

  2. The loop

    The complete physical/digital/accountability loop.

  3. The evidence

    Assets reviewed three times and source-recovery status.

  4. The work

    Work completed during this planning effort.

  5. The maturity

    Current maturity by brand, product, database, operations, finance, physical production, and crowdfunding.

  6. The plan

    The PDR, pilot scope, architecture, roles, tools, skills, subscriptions, risks, release gates, campaign path, and timeline.

  7. The legend

    A clear "done / next / gated / deferred" visual legend.

  8. The links

    Links to every final planning document.

Acceptance criteria

No unsupported numbers or partner claims; semantic HTML; keyboard navigation; WCAG 2.2 AA contrast; reduced motion; responsive from 360px through large desktop; print stylesheet; no external runtime dependency; all links and local assets resolve; HTML/CSS/JS syntax and browser rendering verified.

The through-line: every asset — from a vinyl sticker to a Form C input — gets an owner, a rights check, and an approver before it meets the public. See what these assets run on in 17 — Platforms, Subscriptions, and Services.
