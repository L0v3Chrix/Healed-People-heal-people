This road only moves as fast as trust can walk.

This document serves everyone being asked to wait — each gate exists to protect neighbors, partners, and supporters from a rushed launch.

How we ship: small slices, real evidence

Delivery method · one backlog, vertical slices

Use one prioritized backlog and ship vertical, independently testable slices — every work package ends with review evidence and a release decision.

Parallel work, gated activation

Engineering may proceed in parallel with entity/campaign/partner work, but no real financial or participant-impacting behavior is activated before its professional and operational gates pass.

Phase 0 — Take control before building anything (week 1)

Establish control · one source of truth

Week one is about governance: who owns what, where the code lives, and which advisers are on call.

  • Approve PDR, scope, tech requirements, and authority model.
  • Create authoritative Git baseline; preserve story/static artifacts as references.
  • Pin dependencies; add CI build/lint/typecheck/test skeleton.
  • Create organization-owned GitHub/Vercel/Supabase/password/email workspaces with owners/MFA.
  • Open claims, risk, decision, vendor, service, and access registers.
  • Retain entity/securities, tax/accounting, insurance, privacy, and safeguarding advisers.
Exit criteria

One source of truth, no personal-only production account, no latest dependencies, reproducible clean build, approved backlog, named gate owners.

Phase 1 — Prove the service and the story (weeks 1–4)

Prove the service and campaign story · physical + workflow proof

Before deep engineering, show the whole loop works in the hand and on the page — and that the campaign can be honestly evaluated.

  • Service blueprint and role journeys.
  • Final name/domain decision and brand/rights cleanup.
  • Controlled click-through/demo of pass, scan, reveal, verification, obligation, review, and report.
  • Secure sticker/code specification and physical print proof.
  • Community-partner and business outreach package; letters of interest where available.
  • Entity/cap table and Wefunder Investor Warmup profile.
  • Bottom-up budget, SAFE recommendation, risks, perk BOM, campaign page/video storyboard.
Exit criteria

Physical and workflow proof exists; campaign materials distinguish simulated from real; advisers/platform can evaluate the offering.

Phase 2 — Build the transaction foundation (weeks 3–7)

Transaction foundation · the trustworthy core

This phase builds the data model and flows that everything else — money, safety, accountability — depends on.

  • Revised migrations with organizations, roles, consent, custody, audit, idempotency, obligation/execution/correction, support/incident, and claims evidence.
  • RLS and database invariant tests.
  • Authentication/account lifecycle and MFA for privileged users.
  • Operator shell and role-specific navigation.
  • Sticker batch/code export, activation, assignment, custody, void/recall, and audit.
  • Public scan, offer reveal, claim, expiry, and help routes.
Exit criteria

Synthetic end-to-end flow works in staging with denial tests, concurrency/idempotency tests, and no service-role credential in the browser.

Phase 3 — Make redemption verifiable and accountable (weeks 6–10)

Business verification and accountability · one redemption, one obligation

The money question gets its answer here: every effective redemption creates exactly one balanced, reviewable obligation set.

  • Business qualification/onboarding, location/staff access, offer templates, custom review.
  • Authenticated no-POS redemption verification, idempotent replay, dispute/correction.
  • Transactional obligation creation under a versioned simulated/approved formula.
  • Finance review, dual approval, manual execution evidence, failure/reissue, reconciliation.
  • Distributor statement and sponsor aggregate report.
  • Fraud/risk queue and appeal path.
Exit criteria

Automated tests prove one effective redemption creates one balanced obligation set; replay cannot duplicate; corrections preserve history; role access is bounded.

Phase 4 — Ready the people side: safety, access, dignity (weeks 8–12)

Partner operations, safety, and accessibility · pilot readiness

Before any real participant touches the system, the human protections have to be built, rehearsed, and signed for.

  • Participant eligibility/status, training, consent, accommodations, support, complaint, suspension, appeal, and exit.
  • Partner-assisted and printable/no-smartphone flows.
  • Incident and restricted-note controls.
  • Inventory receipt/transfer/return/loss/damage rehearsal.
  • WCAG 2.2 AA remediation and device/low-bandwidth testing.
  • Privacy data map, retention jobs, data-subject request workflow, security/incident tabletop.
Exit criteria

Partner, safety, privacy/security, accessibility, support, and product owners sign pilot readiness; critical scenarios pass rehearsal.

Phase 5 — Launch the campaign and land the financing (parallel)

Campaign launch and financing · earliest after readiness

This phase runs in parallel and starts at the earliest point readiness allows — it funds the beta the rest of the plan describes.

  • Platform-approved issuer, Form C package, SAFE, terms, financial statements, risks, use of proceeds, perks, page, media, FAQ, and communications.
  • Six-week audience warmup and launch readiness.
  • Live Wefunder round, approved updates, investor support, and material-change controls.
  • Post-close funds receipt/accounting and investor reporting.
Exit criteria — and the honest shortfall rule

Usable proceeds cover the approved beta scenario after fees/reserves; finance approves the build/pilot budget. If the minimum is not reached, execute the disclosed shortfall plan rather than silently reducing participant/safety commitments.

Phase 6 — Rehearse everything before it's real (weeks 11–15 or fund-dependent)

Production candidate and pilot rehearsal · full dress run

The production candidate isn't done until the whole loop has been walked end to end with synthetic data and trained people.

  • Production environment, backups/restore, alerts, runbooks, access review, migration/rollback.
  • Final printer run and batch acceptance sampling.
  • Full synthetic dress rehearsal: partner onboarding → pack issue → scan → claim → business redemption → obligation → review/execution evidence → reconciliation → report → dispute/reversal.
  • Business and partner training completion.
  • Support schedule and incident bridge.
Exit criteria

Zero critical/high unresolved release issues; all required approvals and signed agreements active; production smoke and restore pass.

Phase 7 — Start small in Austin, grow only on proof (2–4 weeks)

Controlled Austin beta · one partner, one business, one pack

The beta begins deliberately tiny and earns each expansion.

  1. Start with the smallest real cohort

    One partner, one business/location, one small pack, and a limited active distributor cohort.

  2. Review daily

    Review safety, fraud, support, inventory, redemption, obligations, and system health daily.

  3. Expand only on passed gates

    Expand toward the approved ceiling only after each cohort gate passes.

  4. Learn and reconcile weekly

    Conduct voluntary research and weekly reconciliation.

  5. Claim nothing early

    Publish no impact claim until evidence and privacy thresholds pass.

Phase 8 — Decide honestly: continue, revise, pause, or stop

Exit and production decision · four honest outcomes

The beta ends with a real decision, made against evidence, with all four outcomes on the table.

🟢Continue

Complete loop, reconciliation, safety, usability, business, and partner criteria pass.

🟡Revise

Concept is viable but workflow, offer, support, or economics need bounded changes.

⏸️Pause

Professional, safety, financial, privacy, partner, or technical gate fails.

🔴Stop

No responsible operating model is achievable with available resources.

Whatever the outcome, produce: beta evidence report, remediation backlog, investor update, public aggregate report if approved, production operations budget, and decision on Austin expansion or future city readiness.

The Christmas 2026 objective — and its honest limits

Critical path · objective, not guarantee

The Christmas objective is achievable only if entity/platform preparation, physical proof, partner/business outreach, and engineering proceed in parallel and usable financing arrives by the beta-build decision.

Safety outranks the calendar

The non-negotiable fallback is pilot-ready or controlled-beta status by Christmas with exit in January; the schedule never overrides safety, financial classification, securities, privacy, or partner gates.

The weekly rhythm that keeps it moving

Weekly operating rhythm · plan, deliver, prove, review

A simple cadence keeps the critical path visible and the evidence flowing.

Monday
Critical path, risks, decisions, budget, dependencies.
Tuesday–Thursday
Workstream delivery and test evidence.
Friday
Integrated demo, claims review, release gate, stakeholder update.
Daily during campaign/beta
Support, security, safety, financial, inventory, and platform health review.

What "done" actually means here

Definition of done · the six-part bar

A task is done only when every one of these is true.

  • Its acceptance test passes.
  • Documentation/runbook is current.
  • Accessibility/security/privacy effects are reviewed.
  • Owner and backup can operate it.
  • Evidence is linked.
  • The change is merged through the approved repository process.

Done means someone else could run it tomorrow — and prove it worked today.
