This document serves the delivery team — it names every skill the mission needs so no one is quietly set up to fail.

How to read this matrix

Capabilities, not job titles

The supplied concept already demonstrates the workflows below, so these capabilities must be covered somewhere in the delivery system.

One person can cover many rows

This does not assume each capability requires a separate hire. One person, partner organization, qualified adviser, vendor, or managed service may cover multiple rows after the interview confirms competence, authority, availability, and budget.

Every row starts owner unverified

Current named coverage is not documented in the supplied assets. Every row is therefore owner unverified until the interview records a person or organization.

Record real coverage — the readiness interview fields

What the interview writes down for every row

A skill row moves from unverified to covered only when the interview captures all nine of these fields.

🙋Coverage owner

The named person or organization covering the row.

📜Evidence of competence

Evidence of competence or experience.

🧭Decision authority

What the owner may decide without further approval.

🗓️Availability

Weekly availability and relevant dates.

🎓Training required

Training or onboarding required.

💵Cost and approval

Cost and approval source.

🤝Backup owner

Who steps in when the primary owner cannot.

🏷️Coverage type

Whether the capability is internal, partner-provided, adviser-provided, vendor-provided, or not yet covered.

✅Acceptance evidence

Acceptance evidence demonstrating readiness before beta or campaign launch.

Prove it, don't just read it — the training rule

One rule closes every row

Readiness is demonstrated, never assumed.

The training rule

Training is complete only when the assigned person can demonstrate the workflow against an approved scenario or checklist. Reading a resource alone is not sufficient for money movement, safeguarding, incident response, privacy/security, inventory custody, accessibility, or fulfillment.

People trust what we can show, not what we say we studied.
