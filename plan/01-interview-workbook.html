How every answer is recorded

A — What the beta must prove

Mission, outcome, and beta definition

Before anything gets built, the beta needs one unambiguous definition of success — and this section pins it down.

A1 — Primary beta proof

What exact real-world outcome must occur for Worth Keeping to call the beta successful—who participates, what happens, and at what scale?

Known from assets: the intended loop is sticker handoff → scan/unlock → local redemption → verified event → accountable sponsor-funded support.

Answer: The beta must prove the complete live loop. It must show, end to end, that:

  1. A real sticker connects real people

    A real serialized physical sticker must connect a community distributor to the recipient-facing reward code.

  2. The recipient lands well

    The recipient must enter a polished onboarding/reward experience.

  3. A live offer is redeemed and verified

    A live local offer must be redeemed and verified without requiring a POS integration.

  4. Verification creates accountable value

    The verified event must create accountable participant value.

  5. The result is reportable

    The outcome must be reportable.

Business/sponsor participation and safe community-partner operations are required gates within that proof. Crowdfunding demand is an earlier funding objective used to build the beta and first pilot, not a substitute for operational proof.

The closeout checklist

Interview closeout — what "finished" is required to mean

These nine checks define a complete interview record; they remain open items on the workbook itself.

  • Every question answered, explicitly deferred, or marked not applicable
  • Every provisional answer has an owner and confirmation date
  • Legal/accounting/safety claims are separated from assumptions
  • All illustrative figures remain labeled until approved
  • Conflicts are resolved in a decision ledger
  • Beta success, boundaries, budget, owner, and date are explicit
  • Crowdfunding promise, cost model, rewards, fulfillment, and approvals are explicit
  • Existing tools/accounts are verified rather than assumed
  • Interview participant approves the final interview record

Every blank field above is a promise we refused to fake.
