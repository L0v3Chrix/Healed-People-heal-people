The ideawho asks, who gives

The inversion

We changed who asks — and who gives.

Traditional charity can separate giver and receiver. One person asks; the other decides. Worth Keeping flips it: a neighbor experiencing hard times makes the first move — by giving. No pitch. No pressure. The first move is a gift.

The sticker isn't a token of need. It's original art people genuinely want — and the person offering it begins the exchange as a contributor.

Worth Keeping does not give people their worth. It recognizes it.

The artworth keeping

Worth Keeping: The Austin Founding Collection

Art people would seek out before they know the mission.

Twenty original pieces rooted in Austin — armadillos, moontowers, grackles, springs, and tacos — made to be collected, traded, and kept. Not charity tokens. Not giveaways. Limited-edition artifacts.

Ten of twenty founding pieces shown. Every piece is platform art — never a business ad wearing a sticker's clothes.

How it worksfour beats

The whole exchange

Give. Discover. Redeem. Ripple.

Simple enough to happen on a sidewalk. Careful enough to be worthy of the people in it.

01

Give

A neighbor offers you original art. A gift, first — never a pitch.

02

Discover

A scan reveals the story behind the exchange — and sometimes a local surprise.

03

Redeem

A local business honors it in person. A real visit, a real welcome.

04

Ripple

That verified moment creates support for the giver — reviewed and approved by humans.

The rule that protects everyone A scan alone never moves money. Only a real, verified visit creates support — and people, not algorithms, approve it.
The moneyhonest and boring, on purpose

Where the value flows

A marketing budget becomes a neighborhood investment.

Local businesses already spend money to be discovered. Worth Keeping redirects that same spend into an exchange where discovery happens through a gift.

The pledge

A business commits support

A set contribution for each verified visit — decided up front, capped, and transparent.

The moment

A verified visit happens

Someone redeems in person. Staff confirm it. That moment — not a scan, not a click — is the trigger.

The ripple

Support reaches the giver

Micro-grants and program support, reviewed and released by people under clear rules.

The beta model in one sentence: businesses fund the loop, verified moments move value, and humans stay accountable for every step.
