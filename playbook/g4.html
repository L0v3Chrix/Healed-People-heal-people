CPA / accounting 1 task

  • File annual Regulation Crowdfunding reportsGated

    Complete the annual Reg CF reporting obligation for as long as legally required after the raise.

    ♥ Annual reporting is the multi-year legal promise made to every community investor the day they invested.

    Done when: Each required annual report is filed on time and confirmed with counsel. · source

Campaign production 6 tasks

  • Launch the platform-approved public roundGated

    Open the Wefunder Community Round at the earliest responsible date after all G3 campaign-readiness gates pass.

    ♥ This is the moment the community is invited to own a piece of the mission — it has to be done right.

    Done when: The round is live on Wefunder with platform approval and all campaign-ready acceptance criteria met. · source

  • Run launch support operationsGated

    Operate the campaign day-to-day: monitoring, responding to questions, and executing planned launch support activity.

    ♥ Backers who show up with questions deserve a team that shows up with answers.

    Done when: Launch support is staffed and active for the duration of the round, with inquiries tracked to response. · source

  • Keep investor communications active through the roundGated

    Publish investor updates per the approved cadence, with every claim passing the claims register and no misleading securities communication.

    ♥ Trust built during the raise is the foundation for everything the beta must prove.

    Done when: Updates are published on cadence throughout the round and each is logged in the claims register. · source

  • Publish the first transparent data updateGated

    Publish one transparent update based on verified platform data within the first 48 hours, without manufacturing urgency.

    ♥ Showing real numbers early — good or bad — teaches supporters this campaign tells the truth.

    Done when: The update is published, sourced entirely from platform data, and contains no manufactured-urgency language. · source

  • Publish weekly campaign updates while liveGated

    Publish a claims-reviewed campaign update every week for the duration of the live raise.

    ♥ Consistent honest communication is the ongoing rent the campaign pays on supporters' trust.

    Done when: An update is published for every week the campaign is live, each with a logged claims review. · source

  • Operate the live Wefunder roundGated

    Run the live round: publish approved updates, support investors, and enforce material-change controls.

    ♥ Every investor question answered well and every update kept accurate honors the trust being extended.

    Done when: The round runs to close with all updates approved before posting, investor support handled, and material-change controls followed without exception. · source

Community operations 3 tasks

  • Assign launch-day roles, access, monitoring, support coverage, and update cadenceGated

    Before going live, assign named launch-day roles, confirm everyone's platform access, set up monitoring, schedule support coverage, and commit to a backer update cadence.

    ♥ Launch day rewards teams that decided who does what before the adrenaline hit.

    Done when: A launch-day roster documents roles, verified access, monitoring, support shifts, and the update cadence. · source

  • Run first-48-hours monitoringGated

    Monitor platform questions, support requests, uptime, press/creator responses, misinformation, and material changes for the first 48 hours.

    ♥ The opening window sets the tone; unanswered questions or unchecked misinformation would erode trust fastest exactly when it matters most.

    Done when: A monitoring log covering all six areas exists for the full first 48 hours with every item triaged. · source

  • Run daily campaign-period health reviewGated

    During the campaign, review support, security, safety, financial, inventory, and platform health daily.

    ♥ Daily eyes on the round catch small problems before they become broken commitments.

    Done when: A daily review log exists for every campaign day covering all six health areas. · source

Executive / product 3 tasks

  • Send the approved launch announcementGated

    Founder sends the approved network/email announcement and posts the approved platform link at launch.

    ♥ The people who trusted the project first deserve to hear about launch directly from the founder.

    Done when: The approved announcement is sent and the platform link is posted, both from pre-approved copy. · source

  • Establish the post-close investor update cadenceGated

    Send monthly investor updates through build and beta, then quarterly thereafter unless offering obligations require more.

    ♥ The people who funded the build deserve to watch it happen, not wonder if it did.

    Done when: The cadence is documented and the first monthly post-close update has shipped on schedule. · source

  • Execute disclosed shortfall plan if the minimum is not reachedGated

    If the round misses its minimum, execute the disclosed shortfall plan rather than silently reducing participant or safety commitments.

    ♥ A hard moment handled transparently protects the people the project exists to serve.

    Done when: Either the minimum is reached, or the disclosed shortfall plan is executed and the decision is recorded with investor communication. · source

Finance 5 tasks

  • Budget Wefunder fees from the signed listing agreementGated

    Budget the published 7.9% of a successful Community Round plus annual admin fee, using the current signed listing agreement rather than the webpage estimate; legal/accounting/entity costs budgeted separately.

    ♥ Fee math from the signed contract keeps use-of-proceeds promises accurate to the dollar.

    Done when: The budget reflects the signed listing agreement's fee terms with separate line items for legal/accounting/entity costs. · source

  • Budget fulfillment and postage before physical perks are offeredGated

    Model pick/pack, mailer, postage, tracking, returns, replacements, and tax/customs with a landed-cost model and reserve.

    ♥ A perk offered without a landed-cost model is a promise the campaign might not be able to keep.

    Done when: The landed-cost model and reserve are documented before perks are offered publicly. · source

  • Record commitments only from platform reportsGated

    Finance records investment commitments only from the platform's own reports, accepting no side-channel investments.

    ♥ Off-platform money would break the legal protections every other investor is relying on.

    Done when: Financial records reconcile exactly to platform reports and any side-channel attempt is declined and logged. · source

  • Complete post-close funds receipt, accounting, and investor reportingGated

    Receive and account for proceeds after close and stand up investor reporting.

    ♥ The moment money arrives is the moment stewardship starts being measured.

    Done when: Funds are received and booked, and the first investor report is delivered. · source

  • Pass financing gate: usable proceeds cover the approved beta scenarioGated

    Confirm usable proceeds after fees and reserves cover the approved beta scenario, and get finance approval of the build/pilot budget.

    ♥ Starting a build the money can't finish would betray participants, partners, and investors alike.

    Done when: Finance signs off that net proceeds cover the approved beta scenario and approves the build/pilot budget. · source

Print + fulfillment 2 tasks

  • Set up perk shipping/fulfillmentGated

    Choose manual small-batch or a vendor after quantities are known, with address privacy, labels, tracking, and a replacement owner.

    ♥ Backers' addresses are personal data and their perks are promises — both handled with care.

    Done when: Fulfillment method is chosen with address privacy, tracking, and a named replacement owner. · source

  • Send the full sequence of fulfillment updatesGated

    Send production, delay, shipment, tracking, loss/replacement, and completion updates to every perk recipient.

    ♥ A backer waiting in silence stops being a believer; steady updates keep the relationship whole.

    Done when: Every fulfillment stage has a sent update on record, through completion or replacement for every recipient. · source

Privacy + security 1 task

  • Collect fulfillment addresses only after close, with retention rulesGated

    Collect shipping addresses only after the offering closes, only for required fulfillment, and publish the retention/deletion rules.

    ♥ Supporters' personal data is held in trust, gathered only when needed and let go when done.

    Done when: Address collection opens only post-close, the published retention/deletion policy is live, and no address is used beyond fulfillment. · source

Securities counsel 2 tasks

  • Claims-review every public update while liveGated

    The claims/legal owner reviews every public update containing numbers, partners, participant outcomes, or terms before it posts.

    ♥ Mid-campaign excitement is exactly when an untrue number is most likely to slip out.

    Done when: Every posted update with covered content shows a logged pre-publication review. · source

  • Run material-change communication and reconfirmationGated

    Communicate any material change through the platform and run investor reconfirmation when required.

    ♥ Investors who committed under old facts have the right to reconsider under new ones.

    Done when: Every material change during the raise shows a platform communication and, where required, a completed reconfirmation cycle. · source

Support 1 task

  • Evidence investor relations and support capability before campaign launchGated

    Establish the investor update cadence, inquiry and escalation scripts, and the material-change approval process.

    ♥ Investors who trusted a first-time team deserve prompt, accurate, disciplined communication.

    Done when: Update cadence, inquiry/escalation scripts, and the material-change approval process are documented and approved. · source
