Gate G7start small, stay honest

Austin beta

Small, daily-reviewed, and real — trust grows at walking speed.

16 tasks Gated

Business sales 1 task

  • Verify businesses can verify and correct transactions without POS integrationGated

    Confirm during live operations that business staff can verify and correct real transactions using only the no-POS workflow.

    ♥ The no-POS promise is what makes participation possible for small local businesses.

    Done when: Live-beta evidence shows businesses verifying and correcting transactions without any POS integration. · source

Community operations 5 tasks

  • Run the controlled Austin beta within the approved envelopeGated

    Operate the 2–4 week live pilot: serialized stickers issued through distributors, real recipient scans, live offers, authenticated redemptions, human-reviewed disbursements, and full record reconciliation.

    ♥ This is the whole point — real people, real dignity, real proof.

    Done when: The beta runs within the approved envelope (≤10 distributors, ≤3 businesses, ≤1,000 stickers, one pilot area) and produces the real end-to-end proof. · source

  • Confirm each active distributor completes at least one valid end-to-end loopGated

    Track that every active distributor completes at least one valid loop, or document a non-product reason why not, before any scale-up.

    ♥ One real completed loop per distributor is the honest minimum bar for saying the service works.

    Done when: Every active distributor has a logged valid loop or a documented non-product reason. · source

  • Verify distributors and partner staff understand activity, value, execution, and appealsGated

    Check during the beta that distributors and partner staff can understand their activity, expected value, actual execution, and how to appeal.

    ♥ Transparency only counts if the people it is for can actually use it.

    Done when: Documented checks confirm distributors and partner staff can explain their activity, expected value, execution status, and the appeal path. · source

  • Start beta with one partner, one business/location, one small pack, and a limited distributor cohortGated

    Launch the Austin beta at minimum scope: one partner, one business/location, one small sticker pack, and a limited active distributor cohort.

    ♥ Starting small means any failure hurts the fewest people and can be fixed fastest.

    Done when: The beta is live within exactly the defined scope and the starting scope is recorded. · source

  • Run daily beta review of safety, fraud, support, inventory, redemption, obligations, and system healthGated

    Review safety, fraud, support, inventory, redemption, obligations, and system health every day of the beta.

    ♥ Daily attention is how a small problem gets caught before it touches a vulnerable person.

    Done when: A daily review record exists for every beta day covering all seven areas. · source

Executive / product 2 tasks

  • Expand toward the approved ceiling only after each cohort gate passesGated

    Grow the beta cohort by cohort, expanding only when the previous cohort's gate criteria pass, and never past the approved ceiling.

    ♥ Gated growth keeps ambition from outrunning the team's ability to care for each person in the program.

    Done when: Every expansion step has a passing cohort-gate record, and the cohort never exceeds the approved ceiling. · source

  • Publish no impact claim until evidence and privacy thresholds passGated

    Hold all public impact claims until the evidence standard and privacy thresholds are both met.

    ♥ Overstated impact would spend the credibility the project needs to actually help people.

    Done when: The claims register shows no published impact claim without a passing evidence and privacy threshold check. · source

Finance 4 tasks

  • Reconcile all verified redemptions one-to-one with the obligation formulaGated

    Verify during the beta that every verified redemption reconciles one-to-one with the approved obligation formula and that no unauthorized or duplicate disbursement/value event occurs.

    ♥ Perfect reconciliation is the proof that participant money moved exactly as promised.

    Done when: A reconciliation report shows one-to-one matching for all verified redemptions and zero unauthorized or duplicate disbursement events. · source

  • Hit the 90% execution service window and track all failuresGated

    Measure that at least 90% of approved executions complete within the disclosed service window and that every failure has a tracked resolution.

    ♥ People waiting on real value deserve delivery on the schedule they were told.

    Done when: Beta metrics show ≥90% of approved executions within the disclosed window and every failure resolved with a tracked record. · source

  • Reassess service tiers at 50%, 75%, and 90% of included usageGated

    Monitor paid-service usage during live operations and reassess tiers when any service hits 50%, 75%, and 90% of its included usage.

    ♥ The pilot never gets throttled or surprise-billed because nobody was watching the meter.

    Done when: Usage alerts exist at all three thresholds and each triggered alert produces a recorded tier decision. · source

  • Run weekly financial reconciliation during betaGated

    Reconcile obligations, executions, and inventory weekly throughout the beta.

    ♥ Weekly reconciliation means no business waits long for money it's owed and no discrepancy hides.

    Done when: A balanced reconciliation record exists for every beta week, with discrepancies investigated. · source

Print + fulfillment 1 task

  • Reconcile pilot inventory with no unexplained active-code discrepancyGated

    Reconcile physical and system inventory during and at the end of the beta.

    ♥ Every unexplained sticker is an unexplained risk to the program's integrity.

    Done when: The end-of-beta inventory reconciliation shows zero unexplained active-code discrepancies. · source

Safeguarding 1 task

  • Resolve every critical safety, privacy, financial, and security incidentGated

    Track all beta incidents and drive every critical safety, privacy, financial, or security incident to resolution.

    ♥ The beta cannot be called a success while any serious harm is still open.

    Done when: The incident log shows no unresolved critical safety, privacy, financial, or security incident at beta close. · source

UX + accessibility 2 tasks

  • Run beta research: consent, metrics, observation, and interviewsGated

    Collect consented research data — metrics, observation, and interviews — during the beta per the beta-research workstream.

    ♥ Listening carefully during the beta is how the program learns what participants actually experienced.

    Done when: Consented metrics, observation notes, and interviews are collected per the research plan and ready for synthesis. · source

  • Conduct voluntary participant researchGated

    Run research with beta participants on a strictly voluntary basis.

    ♥ Participants' experiences should shape the product — but only when they freely choose to share them.

    Done when: Research sessions are conducted with documented voluntary consent and findings are logged. · source
