The four questions that pick the path

  1. Must this raise deliver community financial ownership?

    Yes, non-negotiable → regulated investment. Desirable but not required first → sequence a rewards/altruism stage now, keep the regulated round open.

  2. What must the money buy first?

    Physical proof + demo (~$15–50K) → rewards/altruism, optionally + Kiva. Full beta (~$250K envelope) → regulated round, or sequenced stages.

  3. Can we accept scheduled debt repayment before revenue?

    No → Honeycomb/SMBX stay deferred; only Kiva's $15K at 0% is small enough to carry.

  4. Is six-figure compliance overhead acceptable now?

    Entity, counsel, CPA, Form C, annual reports. If not now, the rewards/altruism family is the only responsible immediate campaign.

Doc 18's recommended sequence — one input, not the verdict

Stage 1 · Founding Neighbors

Recognition-first community campaign (Kickstarter-first) funds the physical proof. Optionally + Kiva $15K for production.

Checkpoint · Evidence

A sticker that scans, a demo that runs, letters of interest, a warm consented audience.

Stage 3 · Regulated round

Only if still needed, only on proof — with Wefunder, Republic, and StartEngine re-quoted at decision time.
