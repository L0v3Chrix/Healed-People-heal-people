From this idea to a real Austin beta

The home page is the why. This is the how: 639 tasks across nine gates, each traceable to its planning document. Nothing moves to the next gate until the current one protects the people in it.

The mission in three sentences

Worth Keeping creates dignity-first exchanges through collectible art, local discovery, and meaningful community participation. The exchange begins when a community participant gives first. Everyone contributes; everyone receives value.

The standard for every decision

Does this make the participant look like a valued contributor — or like the object of someone else's generosity? If agency, dignity, and meaningful value aren't present, it isn't Worth Keeping. (Full brand brief: Doc 22.)

The product loop we are building

The public page shows four beats; internally the loop has eight steps, and every one is someone's job:

  1. Mint

    Generate a unique controlled code.

  2. Print

    Proof, scan-test, activate, and pack.

  3. Pass

    A community participant gives the sticker first.

  4. Reveal

    A public scan explains and unlocks.

  5. Redeem

    Authenticated staff verify the offer.

  6. Account

    One verified event creates one obligation.

  7. Review

    Humans approve and record execution.

  8. Report

    Inventory and aggregate impact reconcile.

How to use this playbook

  • Pick your role, open the earliest gate with your name on it, and start at the top.
  • Every task links to its source document — read the source before improvising.
  • Gated means gated: money, counsel, platform, or proof unlocks it — not enthusiasm.
  • Open decisions stay open: the funding decision page lists every family and platform with real fees — nothing is chosen until the team chooses.
  • The reference library holds all 24 planning documents.
