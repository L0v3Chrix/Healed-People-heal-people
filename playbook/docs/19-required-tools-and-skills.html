Stand up the hosted stack in the right order

Hosted-tool setup sequence · eight steps, one order

Accounts come after the entity, secrets go in one place, and nothing touches production until the acceptance check passes.

  1. Confirm the foundation

    Confirm the legal entity and organization email domain.

  2. Create organization-owned workspaces

    Create organization-owned GitHub, Vercel, Supabase, password-manager, email, monitoring, and design workspaces.

  3. Set owners and lock the doors

    Add Chrix plus one backup owner; require MFA; remove unused default access.

  4. Separate the environments

    Create local/preview/staging/production environments with synthetic data outside production.

  5. Configure domains and email

    Configure domains and email authentication.

  6. Contain the secrets

    Store secrets only in the password manager and environment stores.

  7. Record the money and the exits

    Record billing owner, caps, alerts, renewals, processors, export, and offboarding.

  8. Pass the acceptance check

    Run the acceptance check in 17-platforms-subscriptions-services.html before production use.

The smallest team that can carry this responsibly

Staffing recommendation · roles, not headcount

The minimum accountable delivery group covers thirteen roles — people can double up, but never where accountability would blur.

🧭Executive/product owner

Owns the product decision and the promise behind it.

🛠️Technical lead

Owns architecture, review, and the pinned baseline.

💻Full-stack engineer

Builds and tests the vertical slices.

🎨Product/UX/accessibility designer

Designs flows that work for everyone.

🤝Community operations/safeguarding lead

Keeps participants safe and supported.

🌱Partner manager

Owns the community-partner relationship.

🏪Business/sponsor lead

Recruits and supports businesses and sponsors.

🧾Finance/reconciliation owner

Owns the ledger, approvals, and closes.

⚖️Securities/legal/accounting advisers

Qualified professionals for every gate.

🎬Campaign producer/content lead

Produces the campaign story and media.

📦Print/fulfillment owner

Owns proofs, orders, and physical custody.

✅QA/release owner

Owns test evidence and release gates.

💬Support owner

Owns help, escalation, and follow-through.

Where roles may never merge

People may cover multiple non-conflicting roles, but financial creation/approval/execution/reconciliation and safety escalation require explicit separation and backups.

Trained means demonstrated, not read

Training rule · the standard for "ready"

This project's bar for readiness is performance, witnessed and recorded.

Reading a guide is not completion

Safety, consent, finance, inventory, security, accessibility, support, and release skills are ready only after the assigned owner successfully performs an approved scenario or checklist and a reviewer records the result.

We practice before it matters, so we're steady when it does.
