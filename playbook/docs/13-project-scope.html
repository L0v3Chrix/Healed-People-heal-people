Saying no clearly is a gift to everyone who said yes.

This document serves the beta team — a fence around the work means their promises stay keepable.

What we're setting out to prove

Objective

One bounded Austin beta that proves the whole loop — physical sticker to reconciled participant value.

Prepare, fund, build, and validate a bounded Austin Worth Keeping beta that proves the complete serialized-sticker → public scan → live offer → authenticated redemption → accountable participant-value → reconciliation/reporting loop, while establishing the assets and operating readiness needed for a compliant crowdfunding campaign.

The clock we're working against

Delivery horizon

The desired outcome is an exit decision by Christmas 2026 — with an honest fallback if funding runs slower.

Because crowdfunding is intended to finance the beta, the critical path is conditional:

🎯Best case

Campaign preparation and audience warmup run while the prototype, print samples, entity, partner, and business commitments are completed; the campaign closes or provides approved usable proceeds early enough to build and operate a controlled December beta.

🛟Responsible fallback

Reach pilot-ready status or begin the controlled beta by Christmas and make the exit decision in January 2027.

The date never outranks the gates

No safety, securities, financial, privacy, or partner gate may be waived to preserve the date.

What we will build

In scope

Four fronts move together: the product workflows, the physical system, the crowdfunding preparation, and the operating readiness around them.

Product workflows

  • Anonymous-first recipient scan and explanation.
  • Optional consented preference profile for offer relevance; no sale of personal data.
  • Sticker batch creation, secure code export, print QA, activation, assignment, transfer, void, and recall.
  • Distributor onboarding/status, pack assignment, activity view, support, and partner-assisted access.
  • Business/sponsor prospecting, agreement, location, staff access, offer templates, optional custom offer review, funding/status, and aggregate reporting.
  • Offer reveal, claim, expiry, authenticated redemption, dispute, reversal, and correction.
  • Idempotent ledger/obligation creation from verified redemption only.
  • Human-reviewed value/disbursement queue, dual approval, execution evidence, failure/reissue, and reconciliation.
  • Fraud/risk flags, case review, appeal, and closure.
  • Operator, finance, partner, support, business, and auditor views.
  • Aggregate impact reporting with claims approval.

Physical system

  • Printer-ready platform-owned sticker collection.
  • Two-code or otherwise approved front/back identification model after scan testing.
  • Serialized data export and print proof.
  • Controlled pilot packaging and chain of custody.
  • Defect, loss, theft, duplicate, expiry, recall, and unused-inventory procedures.

Crowdfunding preparation

  • Wefunder Community Round feasibility and entity readiness.
  • One approved security instrument; no guaranteed return language.
  • Investor warmup, campaign page, founder/team story, demo, physical samples, use-of-funds budget, risks, FAQs, disclosures, investor perks, and update cadence.
  • A maximum of five investor perks selected from the documented 35-option menu.
  • Separate business sponsorship packages that are not represented as securities or charitable deductions.

Operations and readiness

  • Community-partner agreement and SOPs.
  • Participant consent, training, safety, support, incident, complaint, suspension, appeal, and exit materials.
  • Business agreements, staff training, offer rules, and dispute handling.
  • Claims register, data map, role matrix, runbooks, launch checklist, and beta evaluation.

What we will not build yet

Explicitly out of scope for the first beta

Saying no clearly is how the first beta stays small enough to be safe and true.

  • POS integrations.
  • Automated participant disbursement without human approval.
  • Multi-city operation.
  • International rewards or fulfillment.
  • An unrestricted self-service marketplace for businesses or offers.
  • Selling, brokering, or sharing personal profiles with advertisers.
  • Biometric identification.
  • Cryptocurrency, tokens, or blockchain.
  • Guaranteed income, guaranteed impact, guaranteed investor return, or tax-deductibility claims.
  • A new 501(c)(3).
  • Public participant leaderboards or gamification that pressures behavior.
  • Production Sticker Oracle/AI personalization. The existing Oracle may remain a labeled campaign demonstration only after privacy, content, cost, and fallback review.
  • Direct integrations with public benefits, shelters, law enforcement, or healthcare systems.
  • Scale beyond the approved Austin envelope without a beta exit review.

How we'll know each stage is truly ready

Acceptance criteria

Three checklists — campaign-ready, pilot-ready, and beta success — and every line must hold.

Campaign-ready

  • Eligible issuing entity and cap table confirmed.
  • One security and its risk/return mechanics approved by counsel and platform.
  • Costed minimum, target, maximum, fees, reserve, and use of proceeds reconcile.
  • Physical perks have quotes, proofs, landed cost, owner, and delivery schedule.
  • Every public claim has a source and approver.
  • No generated person, illustrative number, unconfirmed partner, or guaranteed outcome is represented as fact.

Pilot-ready

  • Every active sticker is unique, scan-tested, activated, assigned, and traceable.
  • Every user role has least-privilege access and account recovery.
  • Verification cannot create duplicate financial obligations.
  • Every obligation has an authorized correction/reversal path and immutable audit evidence.
  • Partner staff complete onboarding, safety, incident, consent, inventory, and support rehearsals.
  • Business staff complete offer and redemption rehearsals.
  • Backup/restore, incident response, rollback, and support escalation are exercised.

Beta success

  • Each active distributor completes at least one valid end-to-end loop or has a documented non-product reason for not doing so.
  • All verified redemptions reconcile one-to-one with the approved obligation formula.
  • No unauthorized or duplicate disbursement/value event occurs.
  • At least 90% of approved executions complete within the disclosed service window; all failures have a tracked resolution.
  • Inventory reconciles with no unexplained active-code discrepancy.
  • No unresolved critical safety, privacy, financial, or security incident remains.
  • Distributors and partner staff can understand activity, expected value, actual execution, and how to appeal.
  • Businesses can verify and correct transactions without POS integration.
  • The impact report contains only approved aggregate evidence.

success is a loop that closes honestly — every time.

When we pause, revise, or stop

Stop and change controls

The brakes are designed in advance, so no one has to invent them under pressure.

🛑Immediate pause

Credible participant danger; suspected exploitation; unauthorized money movement; material data exposure; systemic duplicate codes/redemptions; insurance or partner withdrawal; misleading securities communication.

🔧Revise

Usability failure, weak offer utilization, high support load, print defects, or business workflow friction without immediate harm.

⛔Stop

The operating model cannot be made lawful, safe, financeable, or dignity-preserving within the approved resources.

✍️Change approval

Chrix approves product/scope changes; finance, safety, privacy, legal, and partner owners retain veto authority in their domains once named.
