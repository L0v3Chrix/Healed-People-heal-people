What this sheet is for

The compact response surface

This is the compact response surface for the full interview in 01-interview-workbook.html.

The live interview will move one decision at a time so later questions can adapt to earlier answers. Short answers are acceptable; each answer will be expanded into the formal record with owner, evidence, status, and deliverable impact.

Blank fields below are open on purpose — an em dash (—) marks an answer the interview still needs to record.

The one rule of this sheet

No unchecked option is an approved requirement.

Round 1 — Prove the mission is real

Mission and beta proof

Round 1 pins down what the beta must prove, how real it must be, and who it serves first.

1. Primary beta success test

  • Complete live loop: distribution → scan → reward → redemption → verified accrual/disbursement → impact report
  • Sponsor demand — required commercial proof/gate
  • Community-partner operating readiness — required safety/operating gate
  • Crowdfunding demand — pre-beta funding objective
  • Other: —
Qualification or minimum proof

The live loop is the beta's primary operational proof. Crowdfunding comes first to finance product construction, initial stickers, pilot operations, campaign assets/swag, and needed people. Exact minimum volumes and dollar thresholds remain open.

2. Required reality level — what must be real during beta

  • Physical serialized stickers
  • Public scans
  • Live business offers
  • Verified in-store redemptions
  • Real sponsor funds
  • Real participant disbursements/value, subject to correct professional classification
  • Partner-led participant support
  • Public impact report
  • Other: —

3. First people served and first payer

First served, exceptionally well
People experiencing homelessness or financial insecurity who might otherwise beg/panhandle; the recipient scan/reward journey must also be excellent.
First payer or funder
Initially crowdfunding backers; in the operating model, local businesses buying community-centered advertising/sponsorship.

Serve the person on the sidewalk first. Everything else follows.
