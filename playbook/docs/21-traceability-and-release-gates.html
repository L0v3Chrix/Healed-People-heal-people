Why the gates exist

Every gate protects a person — a participant, an investor, a partner, a neighbor. Slowing down here is how the project keeps its promise to them.

Ten rules that keep every document honest

Consistency rules · language discipline across the package

These rules govern what any Worth Keeping document, page, or campaign asset may say — and they bind this plan too.

  1. "Micro-grant," "payout," "pledge," "overhead," and exact splits are placeholders until FIN-01/FIN-02 close.
  2. No named partner, participant, business, printer, adviser, influencer, press outlet, or investor is claimed without permission.
  3. Generated visuals illustrate; they never prove outcomes, people, partners, functionality, or production.
  4. Campaign scenario amounts and percentages are internal models until sourced and approved.
  5. The Christmas date is an objective with a January fallback, not a public guarantee.
  6. Campaign-platform/investment terms use current signed platform documents and counsel approval at launch, not this plan alone.
  7. Beta quantity ceiling is not a launch commitment; gate owners may reduce it.
  8. No scan alone creates participant value.
  9. No personal-data sale is permitted under the current product direction.
  10. The Infinity Page must source status and language from these documents and display "completed," "recommended," "gated," and "deferred" distinctly.
The plainest rule underneath all ten

Say only what is true, name only who has said yes, and promise only what a gate owner has signed for.
