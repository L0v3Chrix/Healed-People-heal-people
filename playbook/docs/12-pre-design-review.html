What Worth Keeping is, in one honest paragraph

Product thesis

A dignity-first loop that turns local advertising spend into accountable community participation.

Worth Keeping is a for-profit, dignity-first civic-commerce platform that redirects a portion of local customer-acquisition spending into accountable community participation. A community distributor gives a collectible sticker to a recipient. The recipient scans the sticker, understands the exchange, unlocks a relevant local offer, and redeems it at a participating business.

The one rule that holds it together

Only a verified redemption may create a financial obligation or participant-value event. The system must show the business, recipient, and distributor what happened — without turning a vulnerable person into campaign content or a unit of advertising inventory.

Commercial customer

What the beta must actually prove

Primary beta proof

The beta succeeds only when the complete physical-to-digital-to-financial loop works with real participants.

  1. Print and issue

    A controlled, serialized sticker is printed, activated, assigned, and issued.

  2. Give first

    A community distributor gives it to a recipient.

  3. Scan and understand

    The recipient scans it and sees a clear, accessible explanation and live offer.

  4. Verify the redemption

    A participating business verifies an actual redemption without a POS integration.

  5. Create one obligation

    The verification creates one idempotent, auditable participant-value obligation under the approved formula.

  6. Human review and execution

    An authorized human reviews and executes the approved disbursement/value process.

  7. Everything reconciles

    Inventory, redemption, obligation, execution, exception, and aggregate impact records reconcile.

Demand is a gate, not a substitute

Business demand and partner readiness are mandatory launch gates within this proof. Crowdfunding demand is the proposed pre-beta funding mechanism, not a substitute for the beta proof.

What every participant receives

Value exchange

Each role in the loop gives something and gets something — and the terms are written down.

🤝Distributor

Begins as the giver, receives transparent verified-activity records, support access, and only the value that the qualified operating model permits.

🎨Recipient

Receives collectible art, a clear explanation, and an optional relevant local offer without being forced to build a behavioral profile.

🏪Business

Receives a controlled offer workflow, verified visits/redemptions, aggregate reporting, and approved attribution.

⚙️Platform

Owns the collectible system, provides operations and measurement, and earns only the disclosed approved fee/overhead/revenue model.

🏘️Community partner

Provides or co-owns recruitment, onboarding, safety, support, inventory handoff, and escalation under a written agreement.

Ten principles the design must keep

Design principles

These are the non-negotiables that shape every screen, sticker, and sentence.

  1. The distributor gives first.
  2. Only verified redemption moves money

    Verified redemption — not a scan, impression, or profile — is the only candidate money-moving event.

  3. Art stays primary

    Platform-owned art remains visibly primary; business attribution must not turn the front of the sticker into an advertisement.

  4. No pity, no savior language

    Public copy avoids pity, savior language, coercion, unverified impact, and implied partner endorsements.

  5. Consent is explicit and granular

    Optional personalization uses explicit, granular consent. Worth Keeping does not sell personal data.

  6. No-POS still means accountable

    A no-POS pilot workflow must still be authenticated, idempotent, correctable, and auditable.

  7. Human review, visible work

    Human review is acceptable in beta; invisible or unaccountable manual work is not.

  8. No story required for access

    No participant must provide a story, image, or testimonial to access the program.

  9. Design for low connectivity

    Low-connectivity and partner-assisted alternatives are part of the service design.

  10. Illustrations are never evidence

    Illustrative values and generated people never appear as operating evidence.

dignity isn't a feature — it's the spec.

What already exists — and what doesn't yet

Existing evidence and maturity

A substantial creative and technical foundation exists; the production system does not.

In hand today

  • Cohesive brand, story system, eight-scene narrative, marketing application, explainer, and technical-roadmap artifacts exist.
  • The main Next.js application and separate story application build from recovered source.
  • Fourteen PostgreSQL/Supabase-oriented tables, RLS policies, seed data, and verification SQL exist as a substantial draft.

Not production-ready yet

  • The transaction product, authorization layer, financial invariants, operator console, partner workflows, business workflows, real print system, and deployed database are not production-ready.
  • The repository needs an authoritative baseline and release history before development begins.

Two standing rules

Segregation rule: no one person may create, approve, execute, and reconcile material money movement.

Claims rule: every public numerical, partner, participant, legal, tax, return, or delivery claim requires a source and named approver.

Seven questions the professionals must answer first

Professional determinations required before public launch

None of these are guesses we get to make ourselves — each needs a qualified determination before public launch.

  1. Participant-value classification

    Correct legal/tax/benefits classification of participant value; “micro-grant” is not yet approved.

  2. Entity and securities

    Issuing/contracting entity, ownership, securities eligibility, capitalization, and Wefunder terms.

  3. Money handling

    Funds custody, revenue recognition, refunds, chargebacks, restricted-use treatment, and disbursement rails.

  4. Participant identity and equity

    Participant identity/KYC, tax reporting, sanctions, benefits interaction, and equitable alternatives.

  5. Austin-specific requirements

    Austin solicitation, promotion, prize/offer, privacy, accessibility, insurance, and field-liability requirements.

  6. Age and consent

    Age and consent rules for recipients and any optional profile/personalization.

  7. Data obligations

    Data retention, processor agreements, incident duties, and consumer rights.

When detailed build work may begin

PDR exit criteria

Three things open the door to detailed build work — and two harder gates guard everything public.

  • This PDR is accepted by Chrix.
  • A source-control baseline exists.
  • The exact beta slice and non-negotiable release gates are represented in the backlog.

Public crowdfunding may launch only after the entity, security, financial disclosures, campaign budget, platform acceptance, claims, risks, artwork rights, and fulfillment plan pass their release gates.

Real participant beta operations may begin only after partner, safeguarding, payment/value classification, privacy, insurance, inventory, support, and incident gates pass.
